Detained Person

The 4 R's Observation

PACE Code C - Annex H sets out the protocol for checking on detained persons in custody who are under influence of alcohol or drugs, or have medical conditions.

The Observation Checklist

1. Rousability

Can the person be woken up? You must use physical contact (e.g., shaking their shoulder) and verbal stimuli. Do not just look through the wicket. If the person doesn't rouse, they need immediate medical attention.

2. Response

When roused, does the person Respond appropriately? Ask them specific questions: "What is your name?", "Where are you?", "What day is it?". Observe if their speech is slurred, coherent, or if they appear confused.

3. Recording

Every check must be recorded in the Custody Record immediately. Include what was said, the level of response, and the exact time of the check. Generic phrases like "looked okay" are insufficient and legally dangerous.

4. Review

The Custody Officer must Review the frequency of checks. If a person's condition is deteriorating, the level of observation must be escalated (e.g., from 30-minute checks to constant supervision or hospitalization).

Critical Warnings

If a person shows any of the following, do not wait for the next check—call for a Healthcare Professional (HCP) or Ambulance:

  • Difficulty breathing or unusual sounds.
  • Inability to follow simple commands.
  • Significant change in skin color or temperature.
  • Evidence of head injury or significant bleeding.
